You know that feeling when you're at home and someone in your family is walking the stairs and immediately you know who it is just based on the creacking sound of the steps? Yeah, making food is the same. You get to know the features of the elements that you are preparing and you get to know how they behave. It is not just with foods but with your kitchenware aswell. For example, I've been making oat porridge longer than I can remember. I make it every single morning and I never, not even once, skip it. I've made it thousands of times. Yet still, when I'm making porridge with oats or kitchenware that I am unfamiliar with, it is very likely that I will somehow mess something up in the process. Happened to me more than on one occasion. I've made porridge in Estonia. I've also made porridge in the Georgia and United States alike. And yet there's always surprises in the process. Wether the stove is gas or electric, or the kettle is teflon or iron, or even if the oats are steelcut, grits, or rolled. Preparing food is about knowing the consistency and features of the ingredients that you are using. It is to get to know their textures, how they mix with eachother and how they react to heat. It is also a date to try how they go with sweet and savory elements and how they act with vinegar.
